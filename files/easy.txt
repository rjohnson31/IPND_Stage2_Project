The CIA triad, comprised of confidentiality, integrity and availability, is a basic model for handling information and/or services.  The three basic components enable information assurers to somewhat systematically consider a number of attributes and/or processes with regard to the information in question.  Confidentiality primarily addresses protecting the privacy of or limiting access to information or services.  Integrity addresses the accuracy of the services, information, data and metadata at different points of existence principle of integrity dictates that the modifications be authorized.  Availability of services, information or data refers primarily to appropriate access and ability to modify by authorized persons.
